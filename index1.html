What's the #OxygenForIndia campaign?

India is facing its toughest health and humanitarian challenge in its history.

With more than 15 million coronavirus cases and 180,000 deaths reported to date, the health system has simultaneously run out of testing kits, hospital beds, and the supply of medical oxygen.
‍
At least 20 states in India are in dire crisis, including New Delhi, the Capital. Despite high-level interventions, lives are being lost every day — simply because hospitals (and in general, states) have run out of oxygen cylinders.

The #OxygenForIndia campaign is an effort to provide lifesaving medical oxygen to those who need it most.

What's the need of the hour?

With no hospital admissions available, the only hope for critical COVID-19 patients to get medical oxygen supply at their homes (in the form of cylinders and oxygen concentrators).

Given the shortage of beds universally, continuous monitoring and medication at home is the recommended course of action from the doctors. Many people recovering at home are in need of oxygen supply.

What can be done?

Despite a capacity to produce at least 7,100 tonnes of oxygen every day, the country is struggling to provide it where it is needed the most due to a supply chain and distribution issue. The delay is a product of where production units are located and a stretched distribution network.

That's where we come in.

Our goal

We are looking to raise $400,000 (approximately INR 30,000,000) to provide 1,500 cylinders and 50 concentrators free of cost — to those who need them but can't afford them.

Each oxygen cylinder costs between $200-$250 (approximately INR 15,000 - 19,000). Contributions are tax-exempt in the United States and India.

Medical oxygen supplies are being provided for free by small-scale industries.

Who we are

Oxygen for India is a group of individuals and organizations from a variety of backgrounds. Special thanks to The Center for Disease Dynamics, Economics & Policy (CDDEP) and to HealthCube, our distribution partner.
